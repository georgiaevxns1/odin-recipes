Carbonara

Description

Classic Italian pasta dish made with spaghetti, eggs, Parmesan cheese, pancetta, and black pepper, creating a creamy, savory sauce without cream.

Ingredients

  • 200g (7 oz) spaghetti
  • 100g (3.5 oz) pancetta or guanciale, diced
  • 2 large eggs
  • 50g (½ cup) grated Parmesan or Pecorino Romano cheese
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste

Steps

  1. Cook the pasta

    Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil and cook the spaghetti until al dente, according to package instructions. Reserve about ½ cup of the pasta water before draining.

  2. Cook the pancetta

    While the pasta cooks, heat a large skillet over medium heat. Add the diced pancetta (or guanciale) and cook until crisp and golden. Optional: lightly sauté garlic for flavor, then remove it.

  3. Prepare the sauce

    n a bowl, whisk together the eggs, grated cheese, and a generous amount of black pepper until smooth.

  4. Combine pasta and pancetta

    dd the drained pasta to the skillet with the pancetta and toss to coat. Remove the pan from heat to prevent the eggs from scrambling.

  5. Add egg mixture

    Pour the egg and cheese mixture over the pasta, tossing quickly to create a creamy sauce. If the sauce is too thick, add a little reserved pasta water, one tablespoon at a time, until smooth.

  6. Serve

    Divide the pasta among plates and top with extra grated cheese and black pepper. Serve immediately.
